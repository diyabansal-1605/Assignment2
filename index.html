What is the longest distance that an aircraft has ever glided?
Friday, August 24, 2001 At around 1 in the morning, Air Transat Flight 236, operated by an Airbus A330–243, departed Toronto, Canada bound for Lisbon, Portugal. Hidden in its right engine was a faulty maintenance repair. During its last routine maintenance inspection, the right engine was replaced with a spare engine of an older model that did not come with a hydraulic pump. As such, the airline allowed the use of a part from a similar engine. The part was an adaptation that did not maintain adequate clearance between the hydraulic lines and the fuel line that was required for the Rolls-Royce Trent 772B-60 engines, which caused the hydraulic and fuel lines in the Number 2 engine to begin abrading together. Eventually, midway over the Atlantic Ocean, a fuel line developed a fracture and began leaking fuel just over four hours into the flight. In the cockpit, the pilots began getting low oil temperature and high oil pressure warnings consecutively on the ECAM, which was a result of higher-than-normal fuel flow through the fuel-oil heat exchanger. Captain Robert Piché believed they were false warnings as the Airbus is heavily sensor-reliant for a lot of its systems. Soon they began getting fuel imbalance warnings on the ECAM. Still believing them to be false warnings, Captain Piché followed standard procedure to balance the fuel out. The pilots opened the cross feed valve, which directly connects via pipes all the fuel tanks so fuel can be transferred. Captain Piché called a flight attendant and asked her to look for fuel spraying from the engine. However, as it was pitch dark outside, even with flashlights she couldn’t see anything. However they left the crossfeed valve open, which allowed all the plane’s fuel to begin leaking out the right engine. Nine minutes later the pilots decided to divert to Lajes Air Base in the Azores and declared a fuel emergency. Almost a half hour later, with still 150 miles to the air base, the right engine flamed out due to fuel starvation. The pilots descended to 33,000 feet to continue flying on one engine. Fifteen minutes later, the left engine flamed out, leaving the massive jet powerless (literally), with only limited systems working. The plane began descending at a 2,000 feet per minute glide. They then glided 75 miles over a seemingly endless 15 minutes to Lajes Air Base, which today remains the farthest distance ever glided by a powerless passenger airliner to a landing. During that time, the pilots seriously considered ditching the aircraft as it was uncertain whether they would reach Lajes or not. They even had passengers put on their life belts, just in case.

Is it okay to only eat two boiled eggs every breakfast?
Eggs are a popular breakfast food that is easy to prepare and are a good source of protein. However, many people wonder if it is okay to eat only two boiled eggs for breakfast, and if it provides enough nutrition to keep them feeling full and satisfied until lunch. The answer to this question depends on various factors, such as an individual's age, gender, activity level, and dietary preferences. However, in general, eating two boiled eggs for breakfast can be a healthy and nutritious choice. One large boiled egg contains about 78 calories, 6 grams of protein, 5 grams of fat, and 1.6 grams of saturated fat. The egg yolk contains essential nutrients such as vitamin D, vitamin B12, and choline, while the egg whites are a good source of protein and contain no fat. Eating two boiled eggs for breakfast provides around 156 calories, 12 grams of protein, 10 grams of fat, and 3.2 grams of saturated fat. This nutrient composition can help to keep an individual feeling full and satisfied until their next meal. In addition, the protein in eggs is considered a high-quality protein, meaning it contains all the essential amino acids that the body needs to function properly. This makes eggs a great choice for people who are looking to build muscle or maintain a healthy weight. However, it is important to note that eating too many eggs can lead to high cholesterol levels in some people. The American Heart Association recommends that individuals consume no more than one egg per day or seven eggs per week to maintain healthy cholesterol levels. Furthermore, it is essential to pair eggs with other nutritious foods to create a well-rounded breakfast. For example, adding vegetables such as spinach, peppers, or mushrooms to the eggs can increase the fiber and nutrient content of the meal. Additionally, pairing the eggs with a slice of whole-grain toast or a small serving of fruit can provide additional carbohydrates and fiber to help keep an individual feeling full and energized throughout the morning. Overall, eating two boiled eggs for breakfast can be a healthy and nutritious choice for most people. However, it is important to consume eggs in moderation and pair them with other nutritious foods to create a balanced meal.
